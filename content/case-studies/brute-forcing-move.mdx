## June: A Stalled Career Path Meets an Eight-Year Goal

I had wanted to move to the US for almost a decade and was in limbo. At this point, I had already been screened out of a number of US private equity interview processes and my current employer wasn't budging, so I took control of the situation myself.

By this time, I really wanted to try an operating role in tech, so decided to give myself a deadline to succeed and put in my 3 months' notice and left.

### Designing my Outbounding System

What began as an attempt to manually craft cold emails quickly became a system when I realised trying to send out 50 emails a day was not scalable. 

I procured Apollo for volume and deliverability, ChatGPT for templates and cross-referencing and five newly created emails (warmed across the way) serving as the backbone for my outbounding. 

![Cold email template screenshot](/figure1-email-template.png "Figure 1: A template I used – on placeholder names, of course.")

### Target Criteria

- Seed to Series C startups
- Founded post-2020 / 2021 (hypergrowth signals)
- Backed by credible investors (Sequoia, a16z, ICONIQ, big tech venture arms, YC / top accelerators for seed-stage)
- Contacts who were decision-makers, not recruiters
- Role focus on strategy, operations, finance, and GTM / sales

### Operating Cadence

- Peak volume of ~250 emails/day
- 10–12 intro calls/week
- 20 minutes prep per intro call
- Iterated sequences whenever reply rate <10%
- Weekends reserved for recovery
- Gmail used to maintain organic deliverability

### Conversion Rules

- If role open, ask immediately for a referral
- If no role open, build relationship and stay in touch
- Many “no role” conversations converted later

## July: Gaining Traction

I woke up a couple days later to three call acceptances and started to take 6-8am calls before my 80 hour work week, and slowly accumulated early interview processes. Concurrently, my previous employer was seeking to move me to the US after I put in my notice period in a bid to keep me there. This process stalled by a full month in between interview rounds, so I dropped out and doubled down on outbound.

By the end of the month, I generated five live interview processes.

## August – September: The Chaos Spike

While in holiday in Japan, a high growth AI hardware company asked me to fly to California on 24 hours notice for final round interviews. I completed a superday, received an offer and flew home with my job locked down - it felt surreal that I could finally remove.

Unfortunately, this was rescinded for company specific reasons and so I had to rebuild this funnel immediately. This time, I launched a second, much larger wave of outreach, generating 12 new active processes by the end of September when my notice period was done. 

## October: Bouncing Back and the Funnel at Full Scale

With my tenure completed at my previous employer, I immediately booked a ticket to the US and left. I had also now progressed a number of these interviews to late stages and needed to close these out. I spent the next month flying between SF and NY and secured 2 offers before accepting my current role and dropping out of all other live processes.

The companies spanned developer infrastructure, AI workflow automation, AI search, fintech, GTM tooling, and analytics. I ultimately chose a Series B analytics company in a RevOps role, because:

- The interview process completed in three days (positive signal for execution rigor)
- Founders had a strong track record in scaling and exiting businesses
- Strong role fit – I was deep into learning about GTM and was looking for a high leverage operating role

## Key Learnings

While these results were fairly strong for a first time outbound funnel, my TOFU volume definitely compensated for mistakes along the way. Some example learnings to keep in mind for conversion rates and broader context down the line:

- Don't accidentally auto-email multiple people at the same company when you're in a live interview process with them
- Simple things: clean company names before you load them into your outbound lists (no suffixes like "Inc", "LLC", "Acquired by") 
- Sometimes you'll get no responses and sometimes you'll get 3 interviews in an hour
- Each introductory call is two-way - it's better to burn an early lead rather than get dragged into a late stage process where there isn't a fit
- Listen to pain points and figure out how your experience can help solve these - strong alignment equals strong fit

- “Unsubscribe” (this was not paraphrased)

## Conclusion: A Job Search Is a Sales Funnel

The fact that this succeeded remains, to date, one of the achievements in my life that I’m most proud of. 

Trying to move countries with no job can feel like an arcane process, but it is effectively a sales funnel, characterised by high volume, low initial probability of success, and a requirement for the seller to put in a lot of grunt work.

For the people that took the time out of their day to call me, thank you. 

For the people I unknowingly spammed, I apologise! 

And for people in my personal life trying to replicate this move, good luck!
